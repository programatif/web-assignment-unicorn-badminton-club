How To Pay

It helps us greatly if payments are made via bank transfer rather than cash. Please get in contact or ask any of the coaches for details.

Adults

The subscription for the 2025/6 season is £105 (£75 for those in full-time education up to first degree level). For players joining part way through the season we will work out a pro-rata amount based on the number of sessions remaining through to the end of the season.

Juniors

We have two groups: 'Beginners' and 'Improvers'. In the Beginners group, the players range from (roughly) 8yrs up to 12 yrs (ie school Year 4 to Year 7, though older players who need to build up their confidence and skills before joining the 'Improvers' group are welcome too). In the Improvers group, the players range from roughly 13yrs to 16+yrs (ie School Year 8 to Year 11/12/13). These age ranges are applied flexibly so that players can work at the level best suited to ensure they can learn effectively, enjoy playing, and progress successfully.

The subscription for players in the 'Beginners' group is £45 for the season; and for those in the 'Improvers' group is £65. We are happy for new players to try out the club to see how they enjoy our sessions; the cost is £3 per session (Beginners group)/£5 (Improvers group) for up to 6 sessions, after which we ask players to take out a full membership (pro rata for the rest of the season and minus the amounts paid so far).

The Beginner sessions are on Thursdays starting at 5:30pm with everyone on court just to play and enjoy the game: the next approx 30 minutes are coached activities aimed at developing good hitting and movement skills: finally we go back to playing and trying to put the learning into practice. The sessions end at 6:30pm.

The Improver sessions are on Mondays starting at 5:30pm with with a brief knock-up and warm-up: this group too work at hitting and movement skills, but with increased intensity and need for consistency and accuracy. We also introduce tactical ideas and positional considerations in doubles. The sessions end at 7:00pm.

Parents
We encourage parents to stay to watch or to assist (for example by hand-feeding/throwing shuttles for players to hit or scoring games). If you find you really enjoy working with players, the club will help you to undertake BE coaching qualifications. We also welcome Year 10 to 13 students as helpers with our youngest players - as well as being fun and challenging in itself, this is also an excellent way of doing the volunteering component of the Duke of Edinburgh Award Scheme at Bronze, Silver or Gold level.

The club takes its safeguarding responsibilities very seriously. The coaches have been DBS checked and have had safeguarding training: our Safeguarding Policy and guidance documents are here.

Visitors

Visitors/occasional players are welcome; this costs £7 a night (£5 for players in full-time education up to first degree level).

If you play as a visitor more than 6 times in the season, we ask you to join as a club member and take out a subscription - anything you have paid as a visitor will be deducted from the subscription due.

We strongly advise players to take out membership of Badminton England which gives you various benefits and insurance covers - click here for more details. The Club's own insurance only covers the Club's liabilities; it does not provide cover or benefits for individual club members or visitors.

Unicorn Badminton Club is a small, friendly club of league and club players, students, junior players and coaches.
